Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our Senior High School programs, admissions, and student life

To enroll in our Senior High School program, you need to submit your completed Junior High School Form 138 (Report Card), a birth certificate, and two valid IDs. You'll also need to pass our entrance examination and interview process.

Consider your interests, skills, and future career goals. We offer career guidance counseling to help you make an informed decision. You can also attend our strand orientation programs to learn more about each track.

We offer academic scholarships for honor students, athletic scholarships, and financial assistance programs for qualified students. Government scholarship programs like ESC and voucher programs are also available.

Strand shifting is possible during the first semester of Grade 11, subject to approval by the guidance office. You may need to take bridging courses to catch up on specialized subjects from your new strand.

Regular classes run from 7:30 AM to 4:30 PM, Monday through Friday. Each class period is 60 minutes with breaks for recess and lunch. Schedules may vary depending on your chosen strand and year level.

Yes! We have various clubs including debate, robotics, arts, music, sports teams, and academic organizations. These activities help develop leadership skills and provide opportunities for personal growth beyond the classroom.
